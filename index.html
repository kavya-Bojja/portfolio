Prediction of Loan Payment

  • Description: Developed a predictive machine learning model to assess the likelihood of borrowers repaying loans, addressing a critical need for financial institutions to minimize lending risks.
  • Technologies Used: Random Forest, Decision Tree, K-Nearest Neighbor, Logistic Regression
  • Features: Utilized the Loan Prediction dataset from Kaggle, comprising 9,578 rows and 14 features. Addressed challenges related to imbalanced data using SMOTE and applied one-hot encoding for categorical variables.
  • Outcome: The Random Forest model emerged as the most accurate, revealing that borrowers with higher FICO scores, lower interest rates, and smaller installments are more likely to repay loans. This model significantly aids financial institutions in making data-driven lending decisions, protecting them from potential monetary losses.
